We are designing a closed-loop, urban household system in a city like Karachi that combines homemade cleaning products, greywater treatment, and urban farming.
Our plan is to produce homemade soaps and cleaners including:
•	Shampoo 
•	Hand wash 
•	Body wash 
•	Laundry detergent 
•	Dishwashing liquid 
The greywater generated from using these products will be treated through a greywater wetland filtration system, and then reused within the same household.
Intended reuse of treated greywater:
We want the filtered water to be safely usable for:
Urban farming & biological systems:
•	Black Soldier Fly (BSF) larvae units (to moisten/soften feed) 
•	Duckweed cultivation (protein production) 
•	Azolla farming 
•	Vermicomposting (earthworms) 
•	Irrigation of creeper vegetables, other vegetables, and fruit trees 
Household reuse:
•	Reuse in laundry and dishwashing (where feasible) 
•	Washing floors, cars, and similar uses 
Key constraints:
•	We are using dry toilets, so no toilet-related reuse is needed 
•	The goal is to reduce freshwater consumption and enable safe reuse in food-related systems (plants, animal feed inputs) 
•	The system must be suitable for dense urban environments (e.g., rooftops) 

What we need from you:
1.	Soap / cleaning product formulations that: 
o	Are greywater-friendly and biodegradable 
o	Have long shelf life 
o	Do not harm: 
	Soil biology 
	Worms (vermicomposting) 
	BSF larvae 
	Aquatic plants (duckweed, azolla) 
o	Avoid buildup of salts, toxins, or harmful residues in reused water 
2.	Guidance on: 
o	Ingredients to use vs avoid (e.g., surfactants, fragrances, preservatives) 
o	Whether different formulations are needed for: 
	Personal care vs laundry vs dishwashing 
o	pH and other parameter considerations for reuse in biological systems 
o	Trade-offs between cleaning performance and ecological safety 
3.	Any design recommendations to improve compatibility between: 
o	Soap formulations 
o	Wetland filtration 
o	Downstream reuse systems

Additional Preferences & Constraints:
•	We prefer formulations that rely on natural, locally available ingredients rather than synthetic or industrial chemicals 
•	The soap and cleaning products should use natural preservation methods (e.g., plant-based preservatives, pH control, drying techniques) to achieve a long shelf life without relying on synthetic preservatives 
•	Priority should be given to ingredients that can be grown locally in Karachi or sourced domestically within Pakistan, minimizing dependence on imports 
•	If certain ingredients are not currently common locally, suggest alternatives that can be cultivated in an urban or peri-urban setting (e.g., rooftop or small-scale farming) 
•	Overall, the system should support self-reliance, low cost, and resilience in a Karachi urban environment
